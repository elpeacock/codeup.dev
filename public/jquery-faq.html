Make the most out of your trip to a National Park

New Rule: Don't be an idiot

Can I feed the animals?
DO NOT EVER feed wild animals. They are wild animals, you will be putting yourself in danger as well as endangering the animals by increasing their reliance on human interaction.
Can I take the animals home?
NO!!!!!!! See this link for more information about what happens when people interfere with wildlife. Do Not Take the Animals home!
Can I hike off trail?
It depends on what National Park you are visiting, however off-trail hiking is not encouraged.Don't be the "Yellowstone Bros" who walked on the Grand Prismatic Spring
Can I camp in a National Park?
Some National Parks have designated campgrounds, these designated campgrounds are the ONLY areas where camping is allowed.
How much does it cost to enter a National Park?
Fees vary from park to park and on the size of your vehicle. Check the fees for the specific National Park you intend to visit in order to be sure
Can I bring my pets?
This varies from park to park, but pets are generally allowed in Campgrounds and on-trail but MUST be on a leash no longer than 6 ft. They are not allowed off-trail.
Do you offer Annual Passes?
The National Park Service does offer Annual passes for individual parks, as well as passes that are good at all National Parks. They also offer a lifetime pass.
What is the largest National Park?
The largest National Park is the Wrangell-St.Elias National Park and Preserve in Alaska at 13.2 million acres.
What is the smallest National Park?
The smallest National Park is the Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial in Pennsylvania at 0.02 acres.
How old is the National Park Service?
The National Park Service was created by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916 and is celebrating it's Centennial Anniversary this year.
